Yield Predictor

Try it here!

Purpose

The purpose of this application is to help farmers predict the total yields for a specific crop that a farmer enters into the application. This web application will aid in the decision of choosing which crops to plant depending on two climate scenarios. They will be able to see predicted yields for the next 10 years and also train the model for another crop in order to compare the results between the two.

How it Works

This application trains a neural network to predict future crop yields based on past and future weather data for Monroe County, New York. More information about the specifics of the neural network can be found on the about page.

Weather data from March-October throughout 2006-2031 was queried from the MACA dataset. The weather data includes Precipitation, Max Temperature, Min Temperature, and Solar Radiation.

For the future weather data, two climate curves were used RCP 4.5 and RCP 8.5. RCP 4.5 represents the low emissions scenario which considers a curtailment in greenhouse gas emissions through greenhouse gas mitigation efforts. RCP 8.5 represents the high emissions scenario which is the worst case scenario if we do not change our greenhouse gas usage. More information can be found here.

Crop yield data used to train the model was taken from the USDA quick stats service. Currently, the application supports prediction for Strawberries, Corn, Snap Peas, and Tomatoes.

The user only has to input the amount of acres they have for the crop they choose in a dropdown. Once the train model button is clicked, the neural network model will be trained and a scatter plot for both RCP 4.5 and 8.5 will be shown for the years 2021-2031. There is also an option for the user to train another model for a different crop, in order to compare the predicted yields.
